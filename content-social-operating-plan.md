# Humanoid Directory — Content and Social Operating Plan

## Decision

Humanoid Directory and the social media operation will be built together.

The website is the trusted source-backed hub.
The social accounts are the distribution, discovery, and community engine.

## Strategic goal

Become the most useful public reference for humanoid robots while building an audience across robotics, AI, automation, investors, engineers, and tech enthusiasts.

## Flywheel

```text
Robotics sources / social accounts
→ source notes and verification
→ robot/company profile updates
→ website articles/news briefs
→ platform-specific social posts
→ audience feedback/corrections
→ better directory data
```

## Primary website content formats

1. Robot profile pages
2. Company profile pages
3. News briefs
4. Demo breakdowns
5. Comparison articles
6. Country/region roundups
7. Use-case pages
8. Weekly roundup: Humanoid Weekly

## Platform roles

### X/Twitter

Role: fastest research, conversation, and discovery channel.

Content:
- breaking updates
- short analysis
- threads
- quote-posts with context
- robot comparison posts
- polls/questions
- replies to robotics community

### LinkedIn

Role: professional credibility and enterprise/investor audience.

Content:
- market updates
- deployment/commercial readiness posts
- founder/company profiles
- funding/partnership summaries
- weekly professional roundup

### Instagram

Role: visual discovery and shareable robotics education.

Content:
- carousels
- robot fact cards
- comparison slides
- timeline graphics
- short demo explainers if media use is safe

### TikTok

Role: mainstream reach.

Content:
- short narrated explainers
- robot demo breakdowns
- top 5 lists
- "what this robot can actually do" clips
- myth vs reality

### Facebook

Role: broader community and cross-post distribution.

Content:
- article links
- short updates
- discussion posts
- group/community sharing where appropriate

### Newsletter

Role: owned audience.

Content:
- weekly humanoid robot race update
- new profiles
- updated specs
- demos
- funding/deployments
- top links

## Editorial standards

- Use sources.
- Separate fact from interpretation.
- Avoid hype words like "game-changing" unless quoted and contextualized.
- Explain what a demo proves and what it does not prove.
- Unknown specs stay unknown.
- Be excited, but credible.
